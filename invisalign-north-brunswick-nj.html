What Is Invisalign?

Invisalign is the world's leading clear aligner system — a series of custom-made, virtually invisible trays that gradually shift your teeth into proper alignment. Unlike traditional metal braces, Invisalign aligners are:

  • Nearly invisible — most people won't notice you're wearing them
  • Removable — take them out to eat, drink, brush, and floss
  • Comfortable — smooth plastic with no wires or brackets to irritate your mouth
  • Precise — each tray is digitally designed to move specific teeth at specific times

Is Invisalign Right for You?

Invisalign is effective for a wide range of orthodontic conditions in both teens and adults, including:

  • Crowded teeth
  • Gaps between teeth
  • Overbite (upper teeth overlap lower teeth too far)
  • Underbite (lower teeth protrude past upper teeth)
  • Crossbite (upper and lower teeth misalign side-to-side)
  • Open bite (teeth don't meet when mouth is closed)

Not sure if you're a candidate? Come in for a free consultation with Dr. Sudha — she'll evaluate your bite, answer every question, and create a plan with no obligation.

How Long Does Invisalign Take?

  • Mild cases: 6–12 months
  • Moderate cases: 12–18 months (most adults)
  • Complex cases: 18–24 months
Wear your aligners 20–22 hours per day for best results. Consistency is the single biggest factor in finishing on schedule.

Invisalign for Teens

Invisalign Teen is specifically designed for younger patients who are still developing. Key features include:

  • Compliance indicators — small blue dots that fade when worn correctly, making it easy to track usage
  • Eruption tabs — space reserved for teeth that haven't fully come in yet
  • Replacement aligners — up to 6 replacement trays included for the inevitable lost aligner

We recommend removing your aligners when drinking hot, sugary, or staining beverages like coffee, tea, wine, or soda. These can stain the aligners and trap liquid against your teeth. Plain water is always fine. Since aligners need to be worn 20–22 hours per day, simply plan your beverage time around meals when aligners are already out.

There may be a slight lisp or speech adjustment during the first few days as your tongue adapts to the aligners. Most patients find their speech returns to normal within a week. Reading aloud at home during the first few days can speed up the adjustment significantly.
